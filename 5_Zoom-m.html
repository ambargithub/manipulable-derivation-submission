Geometric ideal system

Rebuilding the discrete system using coordinates

Scroll/Click on the highlighted portion to sharpen your focus on individual string elements !!

As we zoom in, we find out that \(m_{i}\) is connected to two other mass points, one on each side, by two springs. Lets call these mass points \(m_{i-1}\) and \(m_{i+1}\).

Hover over the mass points to locate them on the x and y axis.

         \(x_{i}\) = \(x\) coordinate of the \(ith\) mass point
         \(y_{i}\) = \(y\) coordinate of the \(ith\) mass point

You would have noticed that once we simplified the model down to the elemental mass points, we also began placing them in a grid of coordinates.

This process, of placing the most simple model element in a coordinate grid, starts the building of a geometric system. This crucial step allows us to gradually build a symbol-based system, and thus build the final symbol-based prediction system that can act out the string's behavior.
